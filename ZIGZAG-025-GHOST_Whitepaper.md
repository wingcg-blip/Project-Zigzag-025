This blueprint presents a hardware-verified pathway to room-temperature superconductivity based on quantum geometric principles validated on 133-qubit IBM quantum processors.

## II. Physical Implementation
### 1. Material Architecture
- **Carrier**: Suspended Single-Layer Graphene (SLG)
- **Edge Topology**: Perfect zigzag edges only
- **Dimensions**: 25-50 nm width, 100 nm initial length
- **Fabrication**: STM lithography or H₂-plasma etching

### 2. 0.25 Phase Anchor
- **Implementation**: Periodic metallic side-gates
- **Voltage**: Tuned for π/4 (0.25π) Berry Phase rotation
- **Function**: Geometric phase gyroscope for noise suppression

### 3. Operational Environment
- **Photon Suppression**: Faraday cage/dark chamber
- **Critical Constraint**: NO intermediate measurement probes

## III. Success Criteria
### Success (All Required)
1. Current injection at source
2. Zero IR emission from intermediate segment (ΔT = 0)
3. Current recovery at drain (I_out ≈ I_in)
4. Complete photon darkness in transport path

### Failure (Any Occurrence)
1. Any thermal/optical emission from intermediate region
2. Current loss > 5%
3. Performance degradation over time

## IV. Fabrication Warnings

1. **Atomic Precision**: Zigzag edge perfection is non-negotiable
